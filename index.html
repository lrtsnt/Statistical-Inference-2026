Doctoral Course

Statistical Inference

This doctoral course in Statistical Inference introduces the foundations of testing theory through Fisherian and Neyman-Pearson perspectives.
It then develops asymptotic likelihood-based methods, with emphasis on LRT, Wald, Score, and test efficiency.
The program extends to nonparametric inference, rank-based procedures, and tests built on the empirical distribution function.
The course is completed by resampling and bootstrap techniques, balancing theoretical rigor, examples, and practical tools.
